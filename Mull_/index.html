Geological Study Sites

Glen More

Ancient Glacial Valley ‚Ä¢ Quaternary Geology

Explore Scotland's geological history through Glen More's spectacular glacial valley. This U-shaped valley showcases textbook examples of glacial erosion, moraines, and post-glacial landscape evolution. The site offers excellent opportunities for understanding ice sheet dynamics and landscape modification over millennia.

Rest Areas Firm Ground Accessible Paths

Loch na Keal Shore

Coastal Geomorphology ‚Ä¢ Marine Terraces

Study marine and terrestrial geological processes along Mull's dramatic western coastline. Examine raised beach deposits, coastal erosion patterns, and the interaction between volcanic bedrock and marine environments. Perfect for understanding sea-level change and coastal evolution.

Coastal Access Shelter Available Vehicle Access

Ardalanish Peninsula

Volcanic Geology ‚Ä¢ Paleogene Basalts

Investigate Mull's volcanic heritage at this spectacular peninsula featuring columnar basalt formations, lava flows, and intrusive igneous features. The site provides insights into Paleogene volcanism and the formation of the North Atlantic Igneous Province.

Firm Surfaces Geological Exposure Scenic Views

Torosay Quarry Complex

Igneous Petrology ‚Ä¢ Gabbro Intrusions

Examine large-scale igneous intrusions in this former quarry site. Study gabbro textures, mineral assemblages, and magma chamber processes. The quarry provides excellent three-dimensional exposure of igneous rocks and contact relationships.

Flat Terrain Clear Exposures Easy Access

Kintra Bay

Sedimentology ‚Ä¢ Coastal Processes

Study modern sedimentary processes and coastal dynamics in this sheltered bay. Examine beach profiles, sediment transport, and the relationships between geology and coastal morphology. Ideal for understanding contemporary geological processes.

Flat Access Rest Facilities Wind Shelter

Lochdon Moraine Field

Glacial Geomorphology ‚Ä¢ Quaternary Deposits

Investigate one of Scotland's finest examples of glacial depositional landforms. Study terminal and lateral moraines, glacial till composition, and evidence for multiple glaciation events. Essential for understanding ice sheet behavior and retreat patterns.

Firm Ground Rest Points Clear Features

Scarisdale River Valley

Fluvial Geomorphology ‚Ä¢ River Processes

Examine river processes, valley development, and fluvial landforms in this accessible Highland stream valley. Study stream profiles, sediment transport, and the relationship between geology and river morphology in a Scottish Highland context.

Day 1: Arrival & Glen More

Travel from Oban to Craignure via CalMac ferry. Settle at Saorphin Farm base. Afternoon fieldwork at Glen More: glacial valleys, moraines, and site orientation.

Day 2: Loch na Keal & Kintra Bay

Morning at Loch na Keal Shore: coastal geomorphology, marine terraces, sediment analysis. Afternoon at Kintra Bay: beach profiles, sediment transport, rest and accessibility survey.

Day 3: Ardalanish Peninsula & Scarisdale River

Explore Ardalanish Peninsula's basalt columns and volcanic geology. After lunch, visit Scarisdale River Valley for fluvial processes and stream access studies.

Day 4: Torosay Quarry & Lochdon Moraine

Detailed igneous petrology at Torosay Quarry Complex: gabbro intrusions, rock strength analysis. Afternoon at Lochdon Moraine Field for glacial deposits and geomorphology.

Day 5: Portfolio & Reflection

Compile field notes, analyze data, complete portfolio tasks, and group presentations. Option to revisit sites or local amenities before departure.
